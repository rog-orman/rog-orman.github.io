A collection of projects of interest where my engineering skill and expereince has been applied.

Rock Climbing Hangboards

Hangboards are a piece of equipment frequently used for training finger strength in rock climbing. Each board has holes of varying depth and finger capacity to isolate certain muscle and ligament groups. I've been rock climbing for over a year and decided that these boards could be both a useful and fun project to test my expertise. I am currently in the process of CNCing a wooden block to create the central model to the right.

Rock Climbing Porta-board

Portaboards have many similar uses to hangboards. However, they are about a third of the size and have holes in them so that they can be hung from tree branches, bars, and such. In the case to the left, I CADed, 3d printed, and then used screws to mount a prototype portaboard to a wall.

Cantilever

This cantilever was a small-scale physics project designed to test truss strength. The highest tested mass was 2kgs which the created structure handled with ease and no visible bending or deformation.
